About

I build systems that
scale to millions

I am a software engineer with 8+ years shipping production software across mobile, desktop, VR, and live service platforms. My work tends to live at the intersection of client and server -- the messy, interesting middle where decisions about data serialization, system architecture, and user experience all collide at once.

Most recently I was at Very Very Spaceship on Pokemon Go, where I led a complete overhaul of the user profile and customization system touching 122.5 million monthly active users. Before that, six years at Smashing Ideas building everything from real-time aerospace training simulations in C++ to companion apps, analytics pipelines, and cross-platform ports -- the kind of work that makes you comfortable with ambiguity and end-to-end ownership.

Outside of engineering I maintain several freshwater tropical aquariums -- a hobby that rewards the same instincts as systems work: patience, obsessive attention to invisible variables, and a deep appreciation for the fact that everything is connected to everything else. I also volunteer as a Program Advisory Member for Sno-Isle Tech, reviewing student game projects and advising on industry direction. It turns out I like building things almost as much as I like helping other people learn to build things.
